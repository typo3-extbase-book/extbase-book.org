In 2016, Patrick and Michael published the TYPO3 Extbase Book the first time at Leanpub. Readers subscribe to an eBook and whenever the authors release a new version, Leanpub ensures that all existing readers receive the update instantly and for free.

Versions vs. Editions

What's the difference between a new version and a new edition?

New versions typically include small updates like corrected typos or fixed issues in source code. They are always free and automatically available to all readers of the eBook at Leanpub. Readers should make sure the email address entered is correct and up-to-date so that Leanpub can notify existing readers when a new version has been published.

A new edition is a new book. The main use case of new editions is to have different books about different versions of a programming language, software or framework. New editions typically only happen every couple of years and are a rework, restructure, or major update of a previous edition. Every printed edition of a book has its own ISBN to distinguish between them and usually the previous edition gets retired once a new edition has been published.
